COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are FDA-approved GLP-1 receptor agonist medications originally developed for type 2 diabetes that have been shown to be highly effective for weight management. They work by mimicking hormones that regulate appetite and blood sugar, helping you feel full sooner and reducing cravings. They are administered as a simple weekly injection.
Clinical studies show average weight loss of 15 to 20 percent of body weight over 12 to 18 months. Most of our clients lose between 20 and 30 pounds, though individual results vary based on starting weight, adherence to the program, and lifestyle factors. During your consultation, we will discuss realistic expectations based on your personal health profile and goals.
The most common side effects are mild and typically occur during the initial dosing phase. These include nausea, mild stomach discomfort, and occasional constipation. Most side effects diminish significantly within the first few weeks as your body adjusts. Our gradual dosing protocol is specifically designed to minimize these effects, and our clinical team is available to make adjustments at any time.
Most clients are on the program for 6 to 12 months, depending on their weight loss goals and individual response to the medication. After reaching your goal weight, we work with you on a structured maintenance plan to help you sustain your results long-term. Some clients choose to continue a lower maintenance dose under our supervision.
Our program is a self-pay service with transparent, all-inclusive pricing. While we do not bill insurance directly, we can provide documentation that you may submit to your insurance provider for potential reimbursement. Many clients find our pricing competitive with or lower than insurance copays for brand-name medications. We offer flexible payment options and will discuss all costs upfront during your consultation.
You may be a good candidate if you have a BMI of 27 or higher, or a BMI of 25 or higher with weight-related health conditions such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or elevated cholesterol. The program is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or for individuals with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2. We conduct a thorough medical evaluation during your consultation to ensure this program is safe and appropriate for you.
